About

Former neuroscience researcher skilled in MATLAB, R, SQL, and Python looking to transition into the data science space. I have been working in research for multiple years, but have recently decided to switch gears and dedicate myself to data science.

Throughout my career as a researcher, I have dealt with various types of data ranging from survey to neuroimaging data. I started off in the Work and Experience lab at Georgia Tech, where I would usually help run experiments, do literature searches, and discuss methodology/statistics for certain experimental designs. At this point, most of the data I dealt with was survey data that I imported from Qualtrics. I also created and maintained a participant database for the lab so that the graduate students had an organized list of participants they could recruit for their experiments. The tools I would most commonly use to deal with data were Microsoft Excel and Access, while also taking some MATLAB courses as electives. Studying at Georgia Tech and working in this lab allowed me to build a foundation of crucial data science skills, such as qualitative/quantitative analyses, statistics, and scientific methodology.

After graduating from Georgia Tech, I decided to join a neuroscience graduate program, which is why I went next door to Georgia State University. While working on my graduate degree, I joined the Imaging Genetics and Informatics lab, where I would delve deeper into working with data. This lab focused on using neuroimaging to explain various psychological phenomena. Neuroimaging is a very data-driven field and, as a result, I had to adapt in order to run practical analyses. I became proficient in R to write custom scripts for statistical analyses and furthered my proficiency in MATLAB since many scripts and programs used for neuroimaging were MATLAB based. I also learned and applied several machine learning techniques, such as SVM and SVR. My projects often consisted of similar responsibilities to those of data scientists. I often dealt with multiple messy clinical datasets that I would have to clean and standardize to make the data more accessible for my projects and others. After processing the data, I would work on scripts to analyze and visualize data, which would then be organized into reports and presentations. During my time in this program, I participated in several projects, attended multiple conferences as a presenter, and wrote several papers.

After graduating with my Master's degree, I started to enjoy the data wrangling aspect more than the research aspect of my job. This led me to eventually decide to leave the PhD program to pursue data science professionally. To this end, I decided to get a few certifications (Microsoft Excel Expert & IBM Data Analyst) to help educate me on the field of data science since I don't strictly have a computer science background. To help develop my technical skills further, I enrolled in and completed the Thinkful Data Science program. In this program, I learned and became highly proficient in Python and SQL. I also dove deeper into machine learning and completed several projects that applied it to real-world data.

If you would like to know more, then feel free to download my resume at the bottom of the page and check out my projects.
